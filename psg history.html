PSG history

In August 12, 1970, the specially created Paris club merged with the Stade Saint-Germain team from the Paris suburb of Saint-Germain-en-Laye. Stade Saint-Germainois, which had existed since 1904, moved from the 3rd French Division to the 2nd that year. The new Paris Saint-Germain club was headed by Guy Cresan and Pierre-Etienne Guyot. At the end of the 1970/1971 season, PSG entered the top division together with Monaco and Lille. In the 1971-72 season, the club took 16th place, and by the end of the season, a financial crisis and related disagreements in the management followed, which led to the division of the club into Paris and Paris Saint-Germain. Paris remained in the top division, and PSG became an amateur team again, and was relegated back to the third division. Soon PSG returned to the elite and never left it again. Paris dropped to the 2nd division in the year of PSG's second entry into the top league.
